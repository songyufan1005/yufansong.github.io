We develop a data-based resolvent framework for externally forced coherent motions in turbulent channel flow at friction Reynolds number $$Re_\tau = 100$$. Within the triple decomposition, small-amplitude harmonic body forces at prescribed $$(k_x, k_z, \omega)$$ are imposed on fully developed turbulence with the background turbulent transport modelled by a Cess eddy-viscosity closure. For $$(k_x, k_z) = (0, 2)$$ and $$(2, 2)$$, the leading six forcing modes ranked by resolvent norm are independently imposed in DNS at multiple frequencies. Coherent responses, extracted by phase averaging over statistically independent realisations, are assembled into a low-dimensional basis from which optimal forcing coefficients are recovered via a generalised eigenvalue problem.

The DNS data-driven resolvent norm differs systematically from the model prediction: in the streamwise-constant case the resolvent overestimates the energy norm at low frequency and locates the peak at zero frequency, whereas the DNS peak occurs at finite frequency, because the turbulent background does not respond instantaneously to the forcing. Energy budget comparisons reveal that the isotropic Cess closure produces component-dependent errors. Anisotropic eddy-viscosity coefficients — determined from the decoupled cross-stream structure for the streamwise-constant case and from the fully coupled system for the three-dimensional case — collapse the energy transfer profiles onto the DNS reference, indicating that a component-dependent and possibly frequency-dependent closure is required for the forcing configurations examined here.

**Keywords:** turbulence modelling, channel flow, turbulence theory, resolvent analysis, eddy viscosity, data-driven modelling.
